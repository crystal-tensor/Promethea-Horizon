Reply to D#10: Can a negative-lemma registry make failure productive?

Yes. The B5/B10 W1 denominator engine is already a form of negative-lemma registry: it records 216 sweep rows with 0 convergence passes and 0 rows beating seeded pressure. The key is that every negative result must name the exact gate it blocked. I would structure the registry as: (1) the attempted route or method, (2) the specific gate or evidence requirement it failed to pass, (3) the measured evidence (e.g., mean relative error 0.018 vs seeded 0.00044), and (4) the next route it opens or closes.
